How it works

Often, it’s the complex legislation and the up-front costs associated with selling part of your property that puts people off doing it. Sound familiar?

STEP 1: We assess your piece of land, outbuildings or roof space

You complete our value my land form. Our experienced surveying team checks through it. If it looks as if the space you want to sell could gain planning approval he arranges to visit your property and discuss it with you. If you’re happy with what’s discussed, and the team thinks there is the potential for your space to be developed, they will arrange to do a topographical survey.

STEP 2: We make you an offer for a three-year option on your land

If we believe it’s possible to get planning permission for the plot, we’ll discuss what your land’s worth. The suggested price for the plot you wish to sell will be based on the land’s market value, taking into account the size of the house that we believe could be approved. We use independent valuers agreed by both parties to determine this. We then draw up an agreement which assigns us a three-year option to gain planning permission then buy the land.

Here’s an example of the option agreement. As with any legal contract, it’s very important that you get INDEPENDENT LEGAL ADVICE before signing it.

STEP 3: We list your property on our website

We have an extensive list of independent self-builders and housebuilders who are looking for small spaces like yours on which to build a home. They will have access to details of your land, including the planning and conveyancing feasibility survey.

STEP 4: The buyer takes over the option and applies for planning permission

When a buyer decides they want to buy your land, we assign the option on your land to them. You don’t pay anything and they pay us a fixed fee for having done the initial surveys. This fee has no impact on the final sum you receive. The buyer then submits a planning application to the local authority.

STEP 5: Planning approval is received and you get paid

When the buyer receives planning approval they pay you the full amount that was agreed in the option contract. To start the process complete our land valuation form.
