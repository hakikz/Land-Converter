One person’s unused land is the
site for another’s new home

We make it simpler for homeowners to sell part of their property for maximised value without paying out any money, and for self-builders and independent homebuilders to buy and develop unused spaces in London and other UK cities.

As city dwellers, we believe that so much more can be done with spaces in our city. With intelligent design and creative thinking these unwanted or neglected spaces can become small yet beautiful homes.

That’s why we started Land Converter – to help make this happen by combining our experience in land surveying and property development.

Our Directors

These are the people you’ll be dealing with at Land Converter. Paul and Philip have been working together for a number of years and are also directors of Cube Haus Bespoke, a bespoke home-building service that marries awkward urban spaces with modular, sustainable homes designed by some of the UK’s leading architects.

Paul worked within the creative idustries for over 25 years before co-launching Land Converter. He previously founded and ran the award-winning creative agency pd3, which specialies in cultural marketing and works with leading UK and international brands such as O2, including the Memory project and iconic architect and designer Jason Bruges and the Sony T5 project with renowned architectural design agenecy Nicolas Alexadner. Paul sold the agency in 2013 and invested his business acumen and creative vision into his passion; combination original design with new processes, materials and technologies to transform unused spaces into beautiful and functional living spaces.

Philip has run his own business for the past 30 years. His career has been dedicated to great design. In the early 90s he founded a snear brand Acupuncture Footwear which sold in 40 countries, and he is a director of his wife's international luxury fashion label Roksanda, which he helped launch in 2015. Working alongside award-winning architects, Philip has built two renowned houses in central London - The Lost House in Kings Cross by renowned architecture pracitce Adjaye Associates and the Cube House in Clerkenwell. He champions the technology which has made it far eaiser to create high-end, factory-built hourses in steel and timber.
